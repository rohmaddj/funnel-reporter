Needle Movers — Priority Order

CRITICAL #1 — Checkout-to-purchase: /d/ landing flows observed

The /d/ landing page drives the two highest-volume flows (3,367 + 3,518 views) but checkout-to-purchase rates of 8.05% and 5.43% are critically below the 15% floor. Contrast this with /d-v3/ achieving 15.65% and 18.64% on similar offers — confirming the issue is specific to the /d/ variant audience or a segment mismatch, not the offer itself.

  • Audit the /d/ landing page copy and traffic source targeting to identify why its audience is less purchase-ready than /d-v3/ despite similar optin rates
  • A/B test the /d/ landing page with the /d-v3/ copy or structure to attempt to lift checkout conversion to parity (~15%)
  • Consider temporarily reducing ad spend on the /d/ path and reallocating budget to /d-v3/ while testing, to protect revenue
Revenue impact: Lifting /d/ → /offer/v4/ from 8.05% to 15% on 696 checkout clicks = ~48 additional sales × $37 AOV = ~$1,776 incremental frontend revenue per week; /d/ → /offer/v4-v2 from 5.43% to 15% on 718 clicks = ~69 additional sales = ~$2,553 per week.
CRITICAL #2 — Checkout-to-purchase: /e/ landing flows — near-zero conversion observed

Campaign 118 (/e/ landing, 2,556 views) generated only 6 total CPV conversions and a 0.46% checkout-to-purchase on v4 and 2.54% on v4-v2 — both critically below benchmark and dramatically underperforming all other flows. This is the weakest campaign by conversion rate and is likely a traffic quality or audience-offer mismatch issue.

  • Pause or sharply reduce campaign 118 traffic immediately to stop burning checkout clicks at near-zero purchase rate
  • Investigate the /e/ audience segment — compare demographics, interests, or email list quality versus /d-v3/ which performs well
  • If retesting /e/, route to /d-v3/ landing page copy first to isolate whether the landing page or the audience segment is the root cause
Revenue impact: 415 checkout clicks at 0.46–2.54% vs. a 15% target represents ~51–60 lost sales per week (~$1,887–$2,220 in unrealised frontend revenue) — fixing or pausing this flow has immediate ROI.
HIGH #3 — OTO2 Timeline take rate — below critical threshold observed

OTO2 Timeline take rate of 9.4% is critically below the 15% minimum, and the OTO2 downsell is absorbing 13 of the 29 total OTO2 conversions (44.8% of OTO2 revenue comes via the discounted path) — signalling buyers are rejecting the full-price offer but still want the product at a lower price.

  • Revise the OTO2 VSL or sales copy to strengthen the value proposition and urgency before the price is revealed
  • Test increasing the OTO2 downsell price from $37 to $42 to capture more value from the high downsell acceptance rate
  • Add social proof, testimonials, or a guarantee specific to the Timeline product to address purchase hesitation at full price
Revenue impact: Lifting OTO2 from 9.4% to 15% on 171 buyers = ~9 additional full-price OTO2 sales × $47 = ~$423/week; shifting 5 of the current downsell buyers to full price adds another ~$50 — combined ~$470/week backend uplift.
HIGH #4 — OTO1 Blueprint take rate — below strong threshold observed

OTO1 at 27.5% is average but 12.5 percentage points below the 40% strong benchmark; with 171 frontend buyers, reaching 40% would add ~21 more OTO1 sales, and the extremely low OTO1 downsell acceptance (2.9%, only 5 sales) suggests the downsell page is not effectively catching buyers who rejected the full-price OTO1.

  • Test a stronger OTO1 page hook and headline — lead with the #1 outcome/transformation the Blueprint delivers rather than product features
  • Revise the OTO1 downsell page with more compelling urgency copy and a clearer value differentiation from the full-price offer
  • Add a video or animated demonstration of the Blueprint value on the OTO1 page to increase perceived value
Revenue impact: Lifting OTO1 from 27.5% to 35% on 171 buyers = ~13 additional sales × $67 = ~$871/week; improving downsell from 2.9% to 8% = ~9 more downsell sales × $47 = ~$423/week.
MEDIUM #5 — Scale /d-v3/ landing variant — strongest converting flow inferred

/d-v3/ → /offer/v4-v2 delivers 32.99% optin rate, 91.47% offer-to-checkout, and 18.64% checkout-to-purchase — the best performing complete funnel path this week — yet it received only 391 views versus 3,518 for /d/, suggesting significant headroom to reallocate budget.

  • Increase traffic allocation to /d-v3/ (campaign 109) progressively while monitoring CPL and checkout-to-purchase stability
  • Ensure campaign 109 is not capacity-constrained by list size — if email list segment is exhausted, explore expanding to a similar audience
  • Run /d-v3/ alongside /d/ in a deliberate volume test to confirm performance differential holds at higher traffic volumes before full budget shift
Revenue impact: If /d-v3/ → /offer/v4-v2 were to receive the same 3,518 views as /d/ and maintain its 5.63% overall rate, it would generate ~198 sales vs. the ~39 currently produced by /d/ → /offer/v4-v2, a theoretical uplift of ~159 sales × $37 = ~$5,883/week in frontend revenue alone.
