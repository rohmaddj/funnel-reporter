Needle Movers — Priority Order

CRITICAL #1 — Checkout conversion fix observed

Every active flow shows checkout-to-purchase rates of 5–7%, far below the 20–30% target. With 1,635 checkout clicks this week, even a conservative improvement to 12% checkout completion would yield ~88 additional sales ($3,256 frontend revenue) plus backend multiplier.

  • Audit the ClickBank order form for friction: remove unnecessary fields, verify mobile rendering, check load speed and SSL trust signals
  • Add order bump or price-justification copy directly on the ClickBank checkout page (e.g. 'What you get for $37' summary)
  • Test a 3-day urgency or scarcity element on the sales page immediately before the CTA to pre-sell the checkout decision, reducing cold clicks
Revenue impact: Improving checkout-to-purchase from 6.5% to 12% across 1,635 weekly checkout clicks adds ~88 frontend sales ≈ $3,256 frontend + ~$1,500 estimated backend upsell revenue per week.
HIGH #2 — OTO1 take rate improvement observed

OTO1 Blueprint take rate is 29.2% against a 40%+ benchmark; with 113 buyers, closing to 40% means 12 additional Blueprint sales at $67 = ~$804/week in recoverable revenue with no additional traffic cost.

  • Rewrite the OTO1 page headline to lead with the specific outcome gap the Blueprint fills that the core product does not cover
  • Add a short (2–3 min) video or audio testimonial from a buyer who specifically purchased the Blueprint above the fold
  • A/B test reducing OTO1 price to $57 or adding a limited-quantity framing to improve impulse acceptance rate
Revenue impact: Lifting OTO1 take rate from 29% to 40% generates approximately $804 additional revenue per week at current frontend sales volume, scaling proportionally if checkout fix is implemented simultaneously.
HIGH #3 — Fix /offer/v2/ offer-to-checkout rate for /b/ traffic observed

The /b/ → /offer/v2/ flow converts only 60.6% of offer viewers to checkout clicks versus 77–87% for v4-based offers; this gap costs approximately 82 checkout entries per week from this flow alone.

  • Audit /offer/v2/ CTA placement, button copy, and page length versus /offer/v4/ — identify structural differences causing the drop
  • Test replacing the /offer/v2/ CTA section with the v4 CTA block while keeping the rest of v2 copy intact
  • If /b/ landing page traffic is email-sourced, consider routing /b/ subscribers directly to /offer/v4/ to leverage the stronger offer page
Revenue impact: Lifting /b/ offer-to-checkout from 60.6% to 80% adds ~66 checkout clicks/week; at current 5.34% purchase rate that is ~3–4 additional sales ($111–$148 frontend) plus upsell tail — directional uplift.
HIGH #4 — OTO2 take rate and downsell optimization observed

OTO2 Timeline take rate of 16.8% is below the 25% benchmark, and the downsell is nearly invisible at 0.9% — the downsell may have a redirect or presentation issue, and the OTO2 page itself may need urgency or value reinforcement.

  • Verify OTO2 downsell redirect is functioning correctly — 0.9% take rate on a $37 offer for people who declined $47 suggests most decliners are not seeing or engaging with the downsell page
  • Add a 'why you need this now' urgency block to the OTO2 page specific to the timing/timeline angle of the product
  • Test lowering OTO2 downsell to $27 to create a more compelling price contrast with the $47 full price
Revenue impact: Lifting OTO2 take rate from 16.8% to 25% adds ~9 Timeline sales at $47 = ~$423/week; fixing the downsell funnel could recover an additional $27–$37 per declining buyer.
MEDIUM #5 — Investigate and fix /offer (non-versioned) page CTA observed

The /offer page shows 352 offer page views but only 3 checkout clicks (0.85% offer-to-checkout), which is anomalously broken relative to every other offer page and indicates a non-functional or missing CTA rather than a copy problem.

  • Immediately QA the /offer page CTA button link — confirm ClickBank hop URL is correctly embedded and not returning a 404 or redirecting incorrectly
  • Check whether this page is being served to live traffic intentionally (email campaign 92 with 351 views appears to route here) and pause or redirect if broken
  • Once CTA is fixed, assess whether /offer copy is competitive with v4 variants before scaling traffic back to it
Revenue impact: At a corrected 80% offer-to-checkout rate, 352 weekly viewers would generate ~282 checkout clicks; even at the current low 6.5% purchase rate that is ~18 additional sales worth ~$666/week — though this flow may be intentionally low-traffic.
