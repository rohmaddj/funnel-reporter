Needle Movers — Priority Order

CRITICAL #1 — Checkout abandonment fix observed

The main flow sent 1,719 people to ClickBank checkout but only 106 completed purchase (6.17%). Even a modest improvement to 20% checkout-to-purchase would generate ~237 additional sales per week, adding ~$11k+ net revenue weekly at current AOV.

  • Audit ClickBank checkout flow for friction: verify payment methods enabled, test mobile checkout experience, check for cart abandonment triggers
  • Add trust signals immediately before checkout CTA on /offer/v4/: money-back guarantee badge, testimonial snippet, limited-time urgency
  • Implement exit-intent popup on offer page with discount code or reassurance message to recover abandoning visitors before they leave
Revenue impact: $11,000+ per week at 20% conversion, $24,000+ per week at 30% (realistic target for TSL funnel)
CRITICAL #2 — Landing page /a replacement observed

Landing page /a converts at 2.27% to opt-in (vs 13-17% on /b and /c/), meaning 97.7% of traffic is wasted. Campaign 98 sent 44 visitors through this page with zero revenue—immediate traffic reallocation needed.

  • Stop all traffic to landing page /a immediately and redirect Campaign 98 to /c/ (proven 16.97% opt-in rate)
  • Audit /a page for critical issues: form visibility, mobile rendering, headline-offer mismatch, or technical breaks
  • If /a was a test variant, declare it a loss and archive; if production page, replace with /c/ template as emergency fix
Revenue impact: Redirecting 44 weekly visitors from /a (2.27% → 16.97% opt-in) recovers ~6 additional subscribers → ~0.5 sales per week (~$30), but more importantly stops waste on dead traffic
HIGH #3 — Scale winning flow b_c→offer_v4 observed

Flow /b /c/ → /offer/v4/ converts checkout clicks at 39.39% (6.4× better than main flow) with 13 sales from 33 checkout clicks. This flow's checkout-to-purchase mechanics are proven—scaling traffic here bypasses the main flow's critical leak.

  • Identify what's different about /b /c/ → /offer/v4/ traffic source (Campaign 100): audience quality, messaging, traffic temperature—replicate these conditions
  • Increase budget allocation to Campaign 100 (currently 431 views, 16 conversions, 3.71% CR) while maintaining traffic quality
  • A/B test main flow /c/ → /offer/v4/ against /b /c/ → /offer/v4/ checkout page mechanics to isolate the conversion difference
Revenue impact: If Campaign 100 traffic can scale 10× (431 → 4,310 views) at current 3.71% CR, adds ~144 sales per week (~$8,400 net revenue weekly)
HIGH #4 — OTO2 downsell optimization inferred

OTO2 downsell take rate is 1.7% (2 sales from 117 frontend buyers), far below 15-24% benchmark. Either redirect logic is broken (most likely) or downsell offer ($37 Timeline Discount) is not being shown to OTO2 decliners.

  • Verify ClickBank OTO2 downsell redirect is firing correctly: check analytics for downsell page views vs OTO2 declines
  • If redirect is working, test stronger downsell offer: increase discount (e.g., $27 vs $37), add urgency ('one-time offer expires in 10 minutes'), simplify CTA
  • If redirect is broken, fix immediately—every OTO2 decline should see downsell; recovering even 15% of decliners adds ~$500-600/week
Revenue impact: Fixing to 15% take rate adds ~$500-600 per week; optimizing to 25% adds ~$1,000 per week in incremental downsell revenue
MEDIUM #5 — Offer page /offer/v2 CTA strengthening observed

Flow /b /c/ → /offer/v2 has 57.89% offer-to-checkout rate (vs 82-83% on /offer/v4/ variants), losing 42% of qualified opt-ins before checkout. This is a sales copy or CTA design issue, not traffic quality (same landing pages as high-converting b_c→offer_v4).

  • Audit /offer/v2 vs /offer/v4/ for CTA differences: button copy, placement, color, urgency elements—adopt winning elements from v4
  • Add social proof or scarcity elements immediately before CTA on /offer/v2: 'X people ordered today', countdown timer, limited-spot messaging
  • Consider sunsetting /offer/v2 entirely and consolidating all traffic to /offer/v4/ if testing shows no advantage to v2 variant
Revenue impact: Improving offer-to-checkout from 58% → 80% on this flow recovers ~4 checkout clicks per week → ~1 additional sale (~$59), but more importantly validates whether v2 variant should be retired
