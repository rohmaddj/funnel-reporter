Needle Movers — Priority Order

CRITICAL #1 — ClickBank checkout conversion observed

1,720 checkout clicks converted only 106 purchases (6.16%) on the dominant flow, far below the 20-30% target. This indicates ClickBank payment page friction, price shock, or technical abandonment issues.

  • Audit ClickBank checkout flow: confirm payment page is loading correctly, no unexpected fees shown, and mobile experience is functional.
  • A/B test adding a 'What happens next' trust section immediately before the checkout CTA on /offer/v4/ to reduce sticker shock.
  • Implement exit-intent popup on checkout page offering $10 discount code to recapture abandoners (test with ClickBank support to ensure compliance).
Revenue impact: Improving checkout-to-purchase from 6.16% to 20% would add ~238 sales/week × $37 = $8,806 frontend revenue, plus ~$4,400 backend = $13,206 total weekly lift.
CRITICAL #2 — OTO2 Timeline monetization observed

OTO2 Timeline converts at 12.8%, critically below the 15% minimum threshold, with only 15 sales from 117 frontend buyers. Downsell at 1.7% suggests the offer sequence or redirect logic may be broken.

  • Verify OTO2 redirect fires correctly after OTO1 (accept or decline) — audit ClickBank Instant Notification postback URLs and test funnel end-to-end.
  • Rewrite OTO2 VSL/copy to emphasize scarcity and consequence of missing the Timeline reading (current copy likely fails to differentiate from Blueprint).
  • Test swapping OTO2 full price to $37 and downsell to $27 to reduce friction and increase volume (measure total revenue impact, not just take rate).
Revenue impact: Improving OTO2 from 12.8% to 25% would add ~14 sales/week × $47 average = $658 weekly backend lift; fixing broken redirects could unlock the missing 80+ OTO2 impressions entirely.
HIGH #3 — OTO1 Blueprint take rate observed

OTO1 Blueprint at 30.8% is below the 40%+ strong threshold, leaving 10-15 sales per week on the table. With 117 buyers, a 10-point lift would add ~12 OTO1 sales worth ~$804/week.

  • Test reordering OTO1 copy: lead with the 'missing ingredient' angle (what the frontend sketch cannot reveal without the Blueprint) rather than feature list.
  • Add video testimonial showing a customer using Blueprint to unlock deeper insight (social proof at decision moment).
  • A/B test OTO1 price drop to $57 to measure elasticity — volume increase may offset margin compression.
Revenue impact: Lifting OTO1 from 30.8% to 40% would add ~11 sales/week × $67 = $737 weekly; combined OTO1+OTO2 fix could add $1,395/week backend revenue.
HIGH #4 — Split test cleanup inferred

Three low-volume flows (campaigns 91, 95, 98) generated 130 total views, 20 optins, 10 checkout clicks, and 0 sales. These are diluting reporting, wasting analytical focus, and may indicate broken tracking or abandoned tests.

  • Pause campaigns 91, 95, 98 in CPV Labs and redirect remnant email traffic to the proven /c/ → /offer/v4/ flow.
  • Archive /a, /offer, and /offer/v3 page variants to eliminate confusion in analytics and focus optimization efforts on the two working flows.
  • Document learnings from /offer/v3 (35.71% offer-to-checkout suggests weak CTA) before archiving to inform future v4 improvements.
Revenue impact: Cleanup has no direct revenue impact but frees analytical bandwidth to focus on the 99%+ revenue-generating flows; stopping broken tests prevents future wasted ad spend.
MEDIUM #5 — Protect /b /c/ → /offer/v4/ flow observed

This smaller flow (292 views, 14 purchases) achieves a remarkable 41.18% checkout-to-purchase rate, 6.7× better than the dominant c→offer_v4 flow. While sample size is small (34 checkout clicks), the delta is large enough to warrant investigation.

  • Identify traffic source difference: compare campaign 100 (email split test B/C) vs campaign 101 (organic + main email) to isolate what makes /b /c/ traffic convert better at checkout.
  • Hypothesis: /b or /c/ landing page may pre-qualify buyers better or set different price expectations — audit copy differences and consider applying winning elements to main /c/ page.
  • Do NOT scale this flow yet (insufficient sample size) but monitor weekly; if checkout-to-purchase holds >35% over 100+ clicks, consider shifting traffic allocation.
Revenue impact: If /b /c/ checkout conversion pattern (41.18%) could be replicated on the main flow's 1,720 weekly checkout clicks, potential gain is ~602 sales vs current 106 = +$18,352 weekly revenue (hypothesis only, requires validation).
