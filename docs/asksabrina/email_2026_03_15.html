MEDIUM #5 — Variant destiny_v1 vs. destiny_v2 mixed signals

destiny_v1 has higher bounce (61.4% vs. 49.6%), higher choice rate (11.19% vs. 6.06%), higher optin rate (5.84% vs. 3.8%), but lower checkout-from-optin efficiency (26.9% vs. 32.2%). destiny_v2 has lower bounce and better checkout conversion from optin, but worse top-of-funnel engagement. funnel_backend variant_sales shows destiny_v2 with 11 attributed sales vs. destiny_v1 with 2, but this understates true performance because 25 of 38 sales fall into 'other' (unattributed). Session-based signals and attributed-sales signals disagree, making this a mixed read rather than a clear winner.

  • Run controlled 50/50 traffic split between destiny_v1 and destiny_v2 for 2–4 weeks to isolate true variant performance with proper attribution
  • Investigate why destiny_v1 drives higher engagement but lower checkout efficiency: test hybrid approach with v1 engagement mechanics and v2 checkout flow
  • Verify GA4 session attribution is correctly mapping CPV campaign IDs to variant paths, as 66% of sales falling into 'other' suggests attribution leakage
Revenue impact: Cannot be quantified cleanly from this dataset due to attribution limitations. If destiny_v2 checkout efficiency (32.2% from optin) can be combined with destiny_v1 top-of-funnel engagement (5.84% optin rate), a hybrid variant could theoretically lift overall funnel checkout rate by 10–20%, but this is speculative without controlled testing.
